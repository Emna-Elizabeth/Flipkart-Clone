    Tokenisation FAQ
  • Why is my card being tokenised?
    • As per the new RBI guidelines to make card data more secure, merchants like Flipkart cannot store the card details of users. As an alternative, RBI has authorised card networks and card issuers to offer card tokenisation services, which means the replacement of actual credit and debit card details with an alternate code called “token”. The user can either choose to tokenise their card by giving consent for future transactions or choose to continue without tokenisation.
  • What is a token?
    • A token is generated when a user gives consent to Flipkart to tokenise their card. A token is a unique value for a combination of card, token requestor (Flipkart is a token requestor & accepts request from the customer for tokenisation of a card and passes it onto the card network to issue a corresponding token) and device. The token does not contain any personal information linked to your card and is generated only when a customer uses a new card for a successful transaction on Flipkart.
  • Is it safe to tokenise my card?
    • Yes. A tokenised card transaction is considered safer as the actual card details are not shared with the Flipkart during transaction processing . Card information is stored with the authorised card networks or card issuers only and Flipkart does not store your 16-digit card number.
  • Is tokenisation of card mandatory?
    • No, customer can choose whether or not to tokenise their card.
  • What happens if I don’t give consent to secure my card?
    • If you don’t give consent to tokenise your card, you need to enter your card details for every transaction as stipulated under the RBI guidelines.
  • How can I tokenise my card to make it secure?
    • You can tokenise your card by giving Flipkart consent while entering your card details on the “Payments'' page. Consent is recorded when you select the checkbox under your card and complete a successful payment on that card.
  • Do I need to provide consent separately for each card?
    • If you don’t give consent to tokenise your card, you need to enter your card details for every transaction as stipulated under the RBI guidelines.
  • What happens if I don’t give consent to secure my card?
    • Yes, as per the RBI guidelines, you need to provide consent to secure each card separately.
  • Where can my tokenised cards be found?
    • You can find all your tokenised cards in My Account > Cards. You can also find a list of your cards on the payments page while placing an order.
  • Can I delete my tokenised cards?
    • Yes, you can delete your tokenised cards at any given time.
  • Where can my tokenised cards be found?
    • You can find all your tokenised cards in My Account > Cards. You can also find a list of your cards on the payments page while placing an order.
  • How can I delete my tokenised card? (How can I deregister my tokenised card?)
    • You can delete or deregister the tokenised card by visiting the “Cards'' section in the “My Account” page.
  • How can I identify my tokenised cards on Flipkart?
    • You will be able to see your last 4 digits of your card number, bank and network name on Flipkart ONLY if your card is tokenised.
  • Are the new RBI guidelines applicable to both credit and debit cards?
    • Yes, the new RBI guidelines are applicable to both credit and debit cards. Please read the RBI guidelines here.
  • Will I be charged any additional amount to tokenise my card?
    • No, there is no additional charge to tokenise your card.
  • Is there any limit on the number of cards that a customer can request for tokenisation?
    • A customer can request for tokenisation of any number of cards. For performing a transaction, the customer shall be free to use any of the cards registered with Flipkart.
  • How do I make a payment using a tokenised card?
    • You can choose your tokenised card by selecting the “Credit/Debit card” payment option. You can identify the card with the last 4 digits of the card or the card label and proceed with the payment.
  • If I have tokenised a card, will I be able to use a new card for my next payment?
    • Yes. Even if you have tokenised your card on Flipkart, you always have the option to use any other credit/debit card for making a payment.
  • What is a card label?
    • A card label is the name given to your card after tokenising it. This helps in identifying the card at the time of making a payment. You have the option to edit the card label later.
  • How can I edit the card label of a tokenised card?
    • You can add/edit the label of the tokenised card anytime through the “Cards” section in the My Account page on Flipkart.
  • What are the prerequisites for using the tokenised card?
    • To use the tokenised card, the user should have a registered account with Flipkart and should be logged in to their Flipkart account. Due to security reasons, this feature is not available during guest checkout.
  • Can I use a tokenised card to make an EMI payment?
    • Yes. If your tokenised card is eligible for your bank’s EMI program then you can use it for making an EMI payment. You can see the list of available banks by clicking on the EMI option during payment.
  • What will happen to my card which is also stored in the Flipkart account of another friend or family member. Do they also need to secure the card?
    • Yes, the card needs to be secured under all Flipkart accounts individually as per RBI guidelines.
  • Which cards are not impacted by the new RBI guidelines?
    • There is no impact on Bajaj Finserv EMI card (BFL card), and you can continue to save and use these cards as you do today.
  • Whom shall you contact in case of any issues with tokenised card? (Where and how to report loss of device?)
    • All complaints should be made to the card issuers. Card issuers shall ensure easy access to customers for reporting loss of “identified device” or any other such event which may expose tokens to unauthorised usage.
  • Where can more information on RBI instructions on tokenisation be found?
    • The RBI has issued a circular on tokenisation. It is available on the RBI website here.
